Quynh D Ngo Project 3

Yall are sleeping on Anne of Green Gables
Project 3

1. An articulated statement and description of the assignment based on the nature of the requirements.
This was a group project that required creating an interactive ouija board interface using p5.js. The assignment focused on collaborative design, data visualization, and implementing an interactive question-and-answer system based on group-generated questions.

2. Your explanation on the assignment, in terms of the creative act and technical challenges you were asked to engage in
This was a group project that I did with Ariana, Aryel and Puck. It was a ouija board that we based off of deep ocean and aquatic life. We created it based on the questions we came up with as a group, one of which was "would you rather live in a spaceship or under the sea?" which explains the nautical and space themed design. The documented questions can be found in picture 1. I made the bare skeleton of the board with Evee, colored the fish elements as well as put them up the board. It was really nice to actually work with other people on something that doesn't involve any coding, though at the end we had to code it in p5.js.

3. Your Point of view on what was successful and what could be done to improve on it.
Successfully collaborating on both the physical design and digital implementation was a major achievement. The nautical and space theme came together well, and the transition from physical to digital was successful. To improve, I could have more varied types of demonstration of the data, enhance the interactivity, add more visual effects, create smoother animations, or expand the question set.

4. Any additional text observations and additional ideas that help with making your artwork more understandable.
The project demonstrates how collaborative design processes can inform digital interfaces. The deep ocean and aquatic life theme creates a cohesive visual language that connects the physical board to the digital experience. The combination of nautical and space elements reflects the group's question about living in a spaceship versus under the sea, showing how conceptual questions can drive visual design. The ouija board format provides an engaging framework for data visualization, making abstract question-and-answer data more interactive and visually interesting.
